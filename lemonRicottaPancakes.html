Lemon-Ricotta Pancakes

Description

Begin the day with some tasty pancakes for breakfast. These masterful pancakes have their origin from Southern Italy, they were developed by Italian Sephardic Jews, who associated them with Hanukkah. Preparing these Ricotta Pancakes takes 10 mins, you will cook them for 6 mins, then there is an additional 15 min., so expect to spend a total of approximately 30 mins before you are ready to eat them. Lemon-Ricotta Pancakes contains a good amount of calories, up to 554 calories. These delicious pancakes are rich in protein, carbohydrates, cholesterol, sodium plus a few more. They contain everything you need for a healthy breakfast to kickstart your day. They might also make you fat, if you eat too much of them, so be careful. This recipe is perfect for 2 people, so if you are only one person you will be eating for two.

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup cold water or milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 tablespoon grated lemon zest (just the yellow part of the skin)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/8 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 cup self-rising flour
  • 2 tablespoons self-rising flour

Steps

  1. Step 1

    2. Whisk water and baking soda together in a mixing bowl. Add ricotta cheese, lemon zest, vegetables oil, sugar, egg and vanilla. Whisk until smooth, breaking lumps of cheese as you mix. Add melted butter, lemon juice, and 1 cup plus 2 tablespoons self-rising flour. Whisk together, stirring until most of flour disappears into the batter.

  2. Step 2

    3. Let batter sit at room temperature for about 15 minutes.

  3. Step 3

    4. Heat lightly greased cast iron skillet or griddle over medium-high heat. Portion out 1/4 cup scoops of batter onto skillet (cook in batches). When bubbles begin to form on the surface and the edges of the pancakes start to look dry (2 to 3 minutes), flip and cook other side until cooked through, 2 to 3 minutes. Transfer to a warm plate.
